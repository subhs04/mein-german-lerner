🔀 Trennbare Verben

Master German separable verbs with clear rules, common examples, and practical applications. Understanding these verbs is crucial for German sentence structure.

📋 Quick Summary

  • Separable verbs consist of a prefix + verb stem
  • Prefix separates in main clauses, stays attached in subordinate clauses
  • Stressed prefix indicates separable verb
  • Prefix goes to the end of the sentence when separated
  • Past participle: ge- goes between prefix and stem

📋 Rules for Trennbare Verben

1. What are Trennbare Verben?
Trennbare Verben (separable verbs) are compound verbs consisting of a separable prefix and a verb stem. The prefix can "separate" from the verb in certain grammatical situations.
aufstehen = auf (prefix) + stehen (stem)
to stand up, to get up
2. When Does Separation Occur?
The prefix separates in:
• Present tense main clauses
• Simple past tense main clauses
• Imperative forms
Ich stehe um 7 Uhr auf.
I get up at 7 o'clock.
3. When Does the Prefix Stay Attached?
The prefix stays attached in:
• Infinitive forms
• Subordinate clauses
• With modal verbs
• With auxiliary verbs (future, perfect tenses)
Ich muss früh aufstehen.
I have to get up early.
4. Position of Separated Prefix
When separated, the prefix moves to the end of the main clause (before subordinate clauses if present).
Er ruft seine Mutter jeden Tag an.
He calls his mother every day.
5. Past Participle Formation
For past participles, "ge-" is inserted between the prefix and the verb stem.
aufgestanden (auf-ge-stand-en)
stood up / gotten up
6. Stressed Prefixes
Separable prefixes are always stressed in pronunciation. This helps distinguish them from inseparable verbs.
ÁUFstehen (separable) vs. verstèhen (inseparable)
to get up vs. to understand

💡 Practical Examples in Context

🌅 Daily Routine - aufstehen (to get up)

Prefix
auf
Stem
stehen
Present Tense (Separated)
Ich stehe um 7 Uhr auf.
I get up at 7 o'clock.
Modal Verb (Not Separated)
Ich muss früh aufstehen.
I have to get up early.
Perfect Tense
Ich bin um 7 Uhr aufgestanden.
I got up at 7 o'clock.
Subordinate Clause
...weil ich früh aufstehe.
...because I get up early.

📞 Communication - anrufen (to call)

Prefix
an
Stem
rufen

Future Tense (Not Separated)

🛒 Shopping - einkaufen (to shop)

Prefix
ein
Stem

🔄 Word Order Practice

Master the word order rules for separable verbs in different clause types.

Main Clause vs. Subordinate Clause

Main Clause (Separated)
Ich stehe früh auf.
I get up early.
Subordinate Clause (Together)
...weil ich früh aufstehe.
...because I get up early.
Question (Separated)
Wann stehst du auf?
When do you get up?
With Modal (Together)
Ich kann nicht aufstehen.
I can't get up.

💭 Memory Tips
Remember: Separable prefixes are STRESSED!
ÁUFstehen, ÁNrufen, EÍNkaufen - you can hear the emphasis!
Think of it like English phrasal verbs:
"I wake up" → "Ich wache auf" (wake + up = wach + auf)
Prefix position rule:
Main clause = prefix flies to the end! Subordinate clause = prefix stays glued to verb!
