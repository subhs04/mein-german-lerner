üìñ When is Dativ Used?

The Dativ case is used in German in these specific situations. Understanding when to use Dativ is crucial for speaking German correctly. Let's explore each situation with detailed explanations and examples.

1. After Specific Prepositions (Always Dativ)

Certain prepositions in German always require the Dativ case. When you see these prepositions, you must automatically use Dativ for the following noun, regardless of the situation.

2. With Certain Verbs (Dativ Verbs)

Some German verbs always take their object in Dativ case. These verbs describe actions that benefit someone or affect someone indirectly.

3. For Indirect Objects

When you have two objects in a sentence - one direct (what) and one indirect (to whom/for whom) - the indirect object uses Dativ case.

4. To Answer Location Questions (WO?)

When answering "where" questions about location (not movement), you typically use Dativ with certain prepositions.

5. To Answer "To Whom?" Questions (WEM?)

The Dativ case directly answers the question "Wem?" (To whom? For whom?)
