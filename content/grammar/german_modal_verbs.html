üìã Modal Verb Usage Rules

  • 1. Position in Sentence (2nd Position Rule)
    The modal verb always takes the 2nd position in main clauses, just like other conjugated verbs.
    Ich kann heute kommen.
    (I can come today.)
    Du musst das machen.
    (You must do that.)
  • 2. Infinitive at the End
    The main verb (infinitive without "zu") goes to the very end of the sentence.
    Wir wollen ins Kino gehen.
    (We want to go to the cinema.)
    Sie darf hier nicht rauchen.
    (She may not smoke here.)
  • 3. Questions - Modal Verb in 1st Position
    In yes/no questions, the modal verb moves to the 1st position.
    Kannst du mir helfen?
    (Can you help me?)
    M√ºssen wir das heute machen?
    (Do we have to do that today?)
  • 4. Negation with "nicht"
    "Nicht" usually comes before the infinitive at the end of the sentence.
    Ich kann heute nicht kommen.
    (I cannot come today.)
    Du darfst das nicht machen.
    (You may not do that.)
  • 5. Subordinate Clauses
    In subordinate clauses, the modal verb goes to the end, after the infinitive.
    Ich wei√ü, dass ich heute kommen kann.
    (I know that I can come today.)
    Er sagt, dass er das machen muss.
    (He says that he has to do that.)
  • 6. No "zu" with Modal Verbs
    Unlike other constructions, modal verbs do NOT use "zu" before the infinitive.
    ‚úÖ Ich will schlafen. (Correct)
    (I want to sleep.)
    ‚ùå Ich will zu schlafen. (Wrong)
    (Incorrect usage)
  • 7. Modal Verbs Can Stand Alone
    Some modal verbs can be used without an infinitive when the meaning is clear from context.
    Ich kann Deutsch. (sprechen is implied)
    (I can (speak) German.)
    Ich muss nach Hause. (gehen is implied)
    (I must (go) home.)
