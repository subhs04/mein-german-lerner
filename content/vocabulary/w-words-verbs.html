🤔 German W-Words & Essential Verbs

Master German question words and the most important verbs with conjugations, pronunciation guides, and memory tips to accelerate your learning.

🤔 W-Question Words

German Word English Meaning Pronunciation Memory Tip Example Usage
WO
Question Word
 WHERE (location) [voh] 💡 Think: "Where?" = WO Wo ist das Museum?
(Where is the museum?)
WOHIN
Question Word
 WHERE TO (direction) [voh-HIN] 💡 WO + HIN = where + to Wohin gehst du?
(Where are you going to?)
WOHER
Question Word
 WHERE FROM (origin) [voh-HEHR] 💡 WO + HER = where + from Woher kommst du?
(Where are you from?)
WAS
Question Word
 WHAT [vas] 💡 WAS = WHAT (both start with W) Was machst du?
(What are you doing?)
WER
Question Word
 WHO [vehr] 💡 WER = WHO (sounds like "where") Wer ist das?
(Who is that?)
WANN
Question Word
 WHEN [van] 💡 WANN = WHEN (double N) Wann kommst du?
(When are you coming?)

💡 Learning Tips

🎯 W-Word Patterns

Notice how German W-words often start with "W" like English. WO (where), WAS (what), WER (who) - the patterns help with memory!

🔄 Verb Practice

Practice the three auxiliary verbs (sein, haben, werden) daily. They're used in almost every conversation and form the foundation of German grammar.

🗣️ Pronunciation Focus

The "W" in German sounds like "V" in English. Practice saying "voh" for WO and "vas" for WAS until it becomes natural.

📝 Daily Usage

Try asking yourself questions in German using these W-words. "Wo bin ich?" (Where am I?) "Was mache ich?" (What am I doing?)
