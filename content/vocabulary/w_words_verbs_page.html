üí° Learning Tips

üéØ W-Word Patterns

Notice how German W-words often start with "W" like English. WO (where), WAS (what), WER (who) - the patterns help with memory!

üîÑ Verb Practice

Practice the three auxiliary verbs (sein, haben, werden) daily. They're used in almost every conversation and form the foundation of German grammar.

üó£Ô∏è Pronunciation Focus

The "W" in German sounds like "V" in English. Practice saying "voh" for WO and "vas" for WAS until it becomes natural.

üìù Daily Usage

Try asking yourself questions in German using these W-words. "Wo bin ich?" (Where am I?) "Was mache ich?" (What am I doing?)
